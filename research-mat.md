We study how atomic structure controls material behavior. Our work maps strain, defects, interfaces, chemical and structural order/disorder, local symmetry, and evolving atomic environments across energy, electronic, quantum, and structural materials.

## Imaging, Ptychography, and Tomography of Battery Materials

Advanced electron microscopy reveals how battery materials evolve, degrade, and fail across length scales. Operando imaging, atomic-resolution STEM, ptychography, and tomography connect electrodeposition, defects, and structural evolution to performance and lifetime, as in our study [visualizing degradation in anode-free aqueous batteries](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26727).

## In Situ Electron Microscopy of Catalysis

Catalytic function depends on local structure, composition, and interfaces under reaction conditions. In situ microscopy and spectroscopy of [plasmonic AuPd/TiO₂ photocatalysts](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18417) link alloy structure, interfacial adsorbates, and light-driven carrier dynamics to selective chemical conversion.
